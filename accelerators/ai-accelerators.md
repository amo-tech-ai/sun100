## Top AI Accelerators

### Y Combinator

- **Website:** [ycombinator.com](https://www.ycombinator.com/)
- **Type:** Accelerator
- **Specialization:** Broad tech accelerator with strong AI focus; backs hundreds of AI-first startups across verticals
- **Funding Provided:** $125K upfront + pro-rata rights
- **Equity Terms:** ~7% equity
- **Program Duration:** 3 months
- **Location:** San Francisco, CA (in-person)
- **Stages:** Pre-seed, seed
- **Benefits:**
  - Unmatched demo-day visibility
  - Investor network access
  - Structured mentorship
  - Strong track record: Many YC graduates have gone on to raise significant AI funding
- **Application:** Rolling early decisions; Winter 2026 opens now, deadline Nov 10, 2025 (8pm PT)
- **Additional Info:** Iconic accelerator that has backed a large number of AI-first companies across various sectors. Strong AI batch history with 75% AI in recent cohorts. Notable alumni include OpenAI.

### AI2 Incubator

- **Website:** [ai2incubator.com](https://www.ai2incubator.com/) (via Allen Institute)
- **Type:** AI-Focused Accelerator
- **Specialization:** Applied AI/deep tech, AI-native startups
- **Funding Provided:** ~$100K–$200K with option for ~$500K follow-on
- **Equity Terms:** Varies
- **Program Duration:** Flexible/ongoing
- **Location:** Seattle, WA (remote-friendly, AI House option)
- **Stages:** Pre-seed, seed, early research-to-product
- **Benefits:**
  - Built by the Allen Institute for AI
  - Access to AI research labs
  - Technical resources and compute access
  - Institutional partnerships
  - Top researchers, advisors, and compute resources
- **Application:** Rolling admissions (no fixed Dec 2025-2026 windows)
- **Additional Info:** Based in Seattle and founded by the Allen Institute for AI. Focused on building AI-native companies. ~15 teams/year; technical/domain founders; no relocation required.

### Google for Startups Accelerator: AI First

- **Website:** [startup.google.com/programs/accelerator/ai-first/](https://startup.google.com/programs/accelerator/ai-first/)
- **Type:** Accelerator
- **Specialization:** AI, machine learning, GenAI, Cloud technology
- **Funding Provided:** Up to $350,000 in Google Cloud credits (equity-free)
- **Equity Terms:** Equity-free
- **Program Duration:** 10 weeks (North America), varies by region
- **Location:** Global programs (North America, Europe, Asia, Latin America, Africa, Middle East)
- **Stages:** Seed to Series A (or equivalent)
- **Benefits:**
  - Up to $350,000 in Google Cloud credits
  - 30 days of free Cloud TPU access through TPU Research Cloud program
  - Dedicated expert help from Google and industry experts
  - Specialist deep dives: Product design, customer acquisition, leadership development
  - Access to key industry events
  - Technical training and support
- **Application:** Check website for regional cohorts (North America program for U.S. and Canada)
- **Additional Info:** A global series of programs. Each cohort of 10-15 startups comes together to tackle technical challenges through a mix of remote and in-person sessions. Equity-free program offering support to AI and GenAI teams globally.

### NVIDIA Inception

- **Website:** [nvidia.com/en-us/startups/](https://www.nvidia.com/en-us/startups/)
- **Type:** Accelerator Program
- **Specialization:** AI, data science, high-performance computing (GPU-heavy startups), ML infrastructure
- **Funding Provided:** Cloud credits and discounts on NVIDIA hardware/software (no standard cash investment)
- **Equity Terms:** Equity-free
- **Program Duration:** Ongoing membership with tiered benefits
- **Location:** Global, remote
- **Stages:** Idea, early-stage, late-stage
- **Benefits:**
  - GPU and cloud credits
  - Discounted hardware and software
  - Deep Learning Institute training
  - Co-marketing and investor connections
  - Scalable support for AI startups building on NVIDIA's GPU and ML infrastructure
- **Application:** Rolling membership; founders can apply anytime in 2025–2026
- **Additional Info:** Program offers scalable support for AI startups, particularly those building on NVIDIA's GPU and machine learning infrastructure. Backed Runway ML. No equity taken.

## Application Tips

### Before Applying

1. **Research thoroughly** — Understand each accelerator's AI focus, track record, and technical resources
2. **Check eligibility** — Verify stage, technical requirements, and location requirements
3. **Prepare materials** — Technical pitch deck, demo, team bios, AI/ML expertise
4. **Network** — Connect with alumni and AI researchers before applying

### Application Best Practices

1. **Show technical depth** — Demonstrate AI/ML expertise and innovation
2. **Highlight compute needs** — If applicable, explain GPU/compute requirements
3. **Emphasize team** — Highlight AI/ML technical expertise and research background
4. **Be specific** — Explain how the accelerator's AI resources will help your startup
5. **Follow up** — Track application status and respond promptly to technical questions

### Using Trackable Pitch Decks

- Use secure, trackable links (e.g., Papermark) instead of email attachments
- Track investor/accelerator engagement with your AI pitch deck
- Get notified when accelerators view your materials
- Protect your IP with watermarking and access controls

## Notes

- **Equity Terms:** Many AI accelerators offer equity-free options (Google, NVIDIA, IBM) or vary terms by program track
- **Funding:** Some programs provide direct funding, others offer compute credits, cloud credits, or in-kind support
- **Duration:** Programs range from 3 months (standard) to flexible/ongoing (AI2, NVIDIA)
- **Location:** Many programs offer remote-friendly or hybrid options, especially post-COVID
- **Specialization:** AI accelerators vary from broad tech (YC) to highly specialized (AI2, NVIDIA)
